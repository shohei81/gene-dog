## Direction notes

- **Return reason as corporate-vague code**: Camille collapses N.'s vague answer ("the coat, mostly. And he isn't quite right.") into a single bureaucratic phrase — `aesthetic mismatch with the household` — that does not require N. to justify or specify. The phrase covers both coat and disposition without naming either.
- **No reorder on this call**: The Brown reorder (#GD-03891) is placed through a separate channel later. The return call is purely about disposing of the unwanted dog. This separation is more disturbing than a same-call reorder, because the customer doesn't have to think of the new dog while returning the old one.
- **Pronoun asymmetry**: N. uses `him`. Camille mirrors `He returns to the Atelier` only after N. has set the precedent. The customer treats the dog as a being; the house remains neutral until the customer leads.
- **One moment of friction**: `What happens to him? After.` — the single beat that breaks the administrative frame. Camille resolves it with department names and §-numbers.
- **The "we don't require a single reason" line**: The most chilling moment of customer-service reassurance. The system is built to absorb undefined complaints.
