Return Policy

Editions issued by gene dogs are living works. The following provisions govern the return, re-inspection, and onward placement of an edition once it has been delivered into the care of its original purchaser. They are read alongside the Care Covenant and the Bonded Lineage Programme.

§1 Eligibility

A return may be requested within fourteen (14) days of the delivery date recorded on the order. After this period an edition is considered fully acclimatised to its household, and further changes of placement are handled under §6 (Atelier Re-Housing) rather than as a return.

Returns are accepted only for editions whose acclimatisation visit (offered complimentary with every order) has been completed or formally declined in writing. Editions for which the visit is still pending must remain with the purchaser until the visit has taken place.

  • The edition was delivered within the last 14 days.
  • The Lineage Certificate is intact and unaltered.
  • The Charm Set, where opened, has not been worn outside the home.
  • No third-party veterinary procedure has been undertaken without prior notice to the Concierge.

§2 Condition on Return

The edition must be returned in a state consistent with normal household acclimatisation. Coat, dermal layer, foam-bearing structures and temperament are assessed against the original release record (see Quality Inspection on the order page).

Minor variation is expected and does not affect eligibility. Material deviation — that is, deviation outside the tolerance bands published per edition — is documented at re-inspection and may affect the refund issued under §7.

§3 Initiating a Return

  1. 01

    Speak to your Concierge

    All returns begin with a conversation. Your Concierge will discuss the cause of the request and, where appropriate, propose foam recalibration or an extended acclimatisation visit before a return is opened.

  2. 02

    Authorisation

    If a return remains appropriate, a Return Authorisation Number (RAN) is issued and attached to the order. The edition is then scheduled for collection by a gene dogs white-glove courier within four working days.

  3. 03

    Collection

    Editions travel only in climate-controlled cabins, accompanied at all times. Drop-off at third-party carriers is not permitted under any circumstance.

  4. 04

    Re-inspection

    Upon arrival at the Atelier the edition enters a re-inspection window of up to seven (7) working days. The outcome determines refund eligibility (§7) and any onward arrangement under §6.

§4 Foam Tolerance

The most frequent cause of return is the perception that an edition's foam output is lower than expected. The following provisions are intended to set that expectation transparently.

Each Bubble Dog edition is released within a published foam tolerance band, measured under standard atelier conditions. For the 42 A/W release of Bubble Dog — Brown the band is:

Edition tolerance band
0.40 – 0.70 L / hour
Average across the edition
0.54 L / hour
Runway film calibration
0.92 L / hour (controlled studio conditions, not representative of domestic use)

Output is naturally affected by ambient humidity, hydration, rest, and the edition's age in weeks. A reading below the band, sustained over a 14-day observation window and corroborated by the Concierge, constitutes a covered concern. A reading within the band — even if it is below the runway film impression — is a matter of expectation rather than condition and is not, on its own, grounds for a full refund.

We acknowledge that the foam visible on the runway is not the foam visible in the home. The runway is an environment of light, fog, music and motion that no household can reproduce. We state this here so that the gap does not need to be discovered.

§5 Re-Inspection & Restocking

A restocking fee of £ 420.00 applies to all returns. This covers re-inspection, temperament re-baseline, dermal hygiene, and Charm Set substitution where the original set cannot be re-issued. The fee is fixed and does not vary by edition or by reason for return.

Where the re-inspection identifies the edition to be within the published tolerance band, the restocking fee is deducted from the refund and the remaining balance is processed under §7. Where the re-inspection identifies the edition to be outside the band, the restocking fee is waived and the full purchase price is refunded.

§6 Atelier Re-Housing

An edition is a living work. It is not destroyed at the end of a return. The following describes what happens to it.

Editions returned in good condition are entered into the Atelier Re-Housing programme. Three onward arrangements are possible:

  1. Re-Edition resale

    The edition is re-listed through gene dogs Re-Edition (§9) at a price reflecting its prior placement. The original Lineage Certificate is annotated, not replaced.

  2. Atelier residency

    Editions for which a new purchaser is not identified within ninety (90) days reside at the Paris Atelier under the long-term care of the House. They are not re-engineered, not represented as new stock, and remain on the lineage record.

  3. Private placement

    In a small number of cases, editions are placed privately with members of the Atelier staff or with partner households, at no charge. Such placements are recorded and audited annually.

The House does not euthanise editions returned under this policy, and does not authorise any third party to do so. This commitment is documented in the Care Covenant and audited annually by an external veterinary panel.

§7 Refunds

Refunds are issued to the original payment method within ten (10) working days of the completion of re-inspection. The composition of a refund is as follows:

Edition purchase price
Refundable, subject to §5
Charm Set
Non-refundable (see §8)
White-glove delivery fee
Non-refundable
Acclimatisation visit
Not applicable (complimentary)

§8 Non-Returnable Components

The following are not eligible for return or refund under any circumstance:

  • The Charm Set (collar, enamel tags, leash), once unboxed.
  • Bespoke or commissioned editions, including monogrammed lineage certificates.
  • Editions whose Genome ID has been altered, obscured, or transferred.
  • Editions resold or rehomed outside the gene dogs Re-Edition channel.

§9 Re-Edition Resale

Editions may not be resold on the open market. The Bonded Lineage Programme requires that any change of placement after the original sale takes place through gene dogs Re-Edition. This is how the House continues to know where each edition is, how it is cared for, and to whom it belongs.

Resale through Re-Edition does not extinguish the original purchaser's record on the Lineage Certificate. It annotates it.

§10 Care Covenant

Every purchaser signs the Care Covenant at the point of delivery. The Covenant sets out the minimum standard of care expected of an edition's household, the role of the Concierge, the frequency of veterinary review, and the conditions under which the House may exercise its right of recall.

A return is one expression of the Covenant. A re-housing under §6 is another. Both are contemplated by the Covenant and neither carries fault.

§11 Definitions

Edition
A single living work issued under a numbered seasonal release. Each edition has a unique Genome ID and Lineage Certificate.
Foam-bearing dermis
The engineered cutaneous structure responsible for the production of foam at the surface of the coat. Tolerance bands are published per edition.
Charm Set
The non-living accessories accompanying an edition: collar, two enamel tags, leash. Issued and finished at the Atelier.
Lineage Certificate
The bonded document recording the edition's release, all subsequent placements, and any Atelier re-housing.
Concierge
A named member of Client Services assigned to each purchaser for the lifetime of the edition.
Re-Edition
The House's controlled secondary channel through which editions are re-placed.
