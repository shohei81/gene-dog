About this dog

A primitive line. Independent disposition. Two-coat seasonal shed.

The Shiba Inu is bred at the atelier from a closed Mk. II line descended from the Japanese national breed. Independent by design, the Shiba does not seek constant household attention; we recommend the dog for purchasers who value composure over compliance.

Coat shedding follows a seasonal cycle: twice yearly the household should expect coat fall of significant volume. The Mame size is a stabilised smaller variant of the line and is not recommended for households with children under ten.

The first year

What the house provides, and what is asked of the household.

  1. Week 0 · Delivery

    White-glove handover

    Climate-controlled courier. Charm Set boxed separately. Signed by purchaser only.

  2. Week 1 · Home

    Acclimatisation visit

    A Concierge attends the household to settle the dog. The Shiba's preferred resting locations are mapped, not negotiated.

  3. Month 4 & Month 10

    Seasonal coat blow

    Twice-yearly major shed. The house provides a brushing schedule; the household provides the surfaces.

  4. Ongoing

    Concierge on call

    A named Concierge is assigned for the lifetime of the dog. Recall training is supported but not guaranteed by the line.
