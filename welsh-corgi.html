About this dog

Pembroke line. Chondrodysplastic short-leg phenotype is engineered into the line and stable. Annual spinal review under §4.

The Welsh Corgi is bred at the atelier from a closed Mk. II Pembroke line. The chondrodysplastic short-leg phenotype that defines the breed silhouette is part of the line standard; spinal health is monitored annually under §4 of the Care Covenant. The dog is alert, vocal, and retains an instinct to herd small moving objects, including children.

The Royal-line variant traces, sociologically, to the preferences of a single household whose choices shaped the breed's modern proportions. Reservations of the Royal-line are subject to an additional Concierge consultation and a longer placement window.

The first year

What the house provides, and what is asked of the household.

  1. Week 0 · Delivery

    White-glove handover

    Climate-controlled courier. Charm Set boxed separately. Signed by purchaser only.

  2. Week 1 · Home

    Acclimatisation visit & staircase audit

    A Concierge attends the household to settle the dog and inspect staircases. Households with frequent staircase access are advised on ramp installation.

  3. Month 6

    Spinal X-ray

    Intervertebral disc imaging recorded against the Lineage Certificate. Repeated annually for the lifetime of the dog.

  4. Ongoing

    Concierge on call

    A named Concierge is assigned for the lifetime of the dog. The Welsh Corgi line carries a published twelve-month return rate of 9%.
