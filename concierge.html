What your Concierge does

The Concierge is the single point of contact for every interaction with the house, from the first acclimatisation visit to a re-housing under §6.

01 · Acclimatisation

Week-one visit

The Concierge attends the household within seven days of delivery. The dog's preferred resting locations are mapped, the household's exercise plan is confirmed, and any climate or staircase audits required by the line are recorded.

02 · Review

Quarterly attendance

Each quarter, the Concierge attends the household — or invites the household to the salon — for a recorded review. The Lineage Certificate is annotated. Coat index and temperament index are re-measured against the published tolerance bands.

03 · Re-shaping

Coat & temperament

If a dog's coat curl drifts below the published band, the Concierge arranges a re-shape at the atelier. Temperament re-baselines are available for the first twenty-four months; beyond that, an exception under §10 must be requested.

04 · Returns & Re-Housing

§1 — §6 of the policy

The Concierge initiates returns, accompanies the dog during collection, attends the re-inspection at the atelier, and oversees re-housing where applicable. Right of recall, used twice in the history of the house, is also exercised through the Concierge.

How the assignment works

One person, for the lifetime of the dog.

Each dog placed with your household is assigned a Concierge at the time of reservation. That Concierge holds the dog's record for as long as the dog remains with you, and continues to hold it through re-inspection, re-housing, or atelier residency where applicable. A change of Concierge is a deliberate act; it does not happen automatically with time.

If your Concierge leaves the house, your record migrates to a successor assigned by the Lead Concierge. The original Concierge does not retain access to your client number. The Lineage Certificate of each dog records every Concierge who has held it. Average tenure of a Concierge with a client is 4.2 years.
