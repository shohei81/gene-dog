About this dog

A stabilised Mk. II brachycephalic line. Best suited to controlled climates and households able to support quarterly respiratory review.

The French Bulldog is bred at the atelier from a stabilised Mk. II line. Short-faced (brachycephalic) by design, the dog is best suited to households able to maintain a controlled indoor climate; cabin-pressure travel is contraindicated under §4 of the Care Covenant.

The Blue coat is a rare phenotype within the line and is associated with elevated dermal sensitivity; reservations of the Blue variant are subject to an additional Concierge consultation. The Micro size is a stabilised smaller variant; expected lifespan is reduced relative to Standard and is documented per dog at six-week review.

The first year

What the house provides, and what is asked of the household.

  1. Week 0 · Delivery

    White-glove handover

    Climate-controlled courier. Charm Set boxed separately. Signed by purchaser only.

  2. Week 1 · Home

    Acclimatisation visit

    A Concierge attends the household to confirm climate controls and review the household respiratory plan.

  3. Month 3, 6, 9, 12

    Quarterly respiratory assessment

    Brachycephalic Obstructive Airway Syndrome (BOAS) grading. Surgical intervention is covered by the house through year two where indicated.

  4. Ongoing

    Concierge on call

    A named Concierge is assigned for the lifetime of the dog. The French Bulldog line carries a published twelve-month return rate of 19%.
